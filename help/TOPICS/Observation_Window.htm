Observations window

The observations window is accessed by:

  • Selecting Data entry – Observations from the Main menu.

  • Pressing the button on the toolbar.

  • The hierarchy in the left panel shows the surveys, survey events, samples and observations available in the database.

  • Select an item by clicking on it with your mouse, or by moving the highlight bar up and down the list using the <up arrow>, <down arrow>, <PgUp>, <PgDn>, <Home> and <End> keys.

  • The details of a surveys, survey events, samples or observations are shown in the right panel. The detail panel changes appropriately, depending on the type of selected item.

TIP: All names are initially displayed in the hierarchy with a button. If you click on an item which has nothing nested below it, the will disappear.

Note: Surveys in the observation hierarchy can be organised into folders by assigning one or more Tags to them. This facility becomes available when Organise Surveys by Tag is ticked on the General tab in Tools - Options. The folders appear as the top level in the hierarchy and the folder names are the tags. The Tags can be created and assigned to surveys in the observation hierarchy. They can also be created via Tools - Enhanced Termlists. Tags enable groups of surveys to be exported and included in reports more easily.

Note: You will not be able to see all surveys in the observation hierarchy if your access to surveys has been restricted - see User Configuration.

How do you decide what type of item to add?

  • To add a new item

    • Select Edit – Add from the Main menu.

    • Choose [Add] at the bottom of the left-hand list.

    • Click with the right side (secondary) mouse button (the one you use least), and a popup-menu will appear. Select 'Add' from this menu.

  • In each case, a submenu will appear asking you what type of item to add. The options will be the type of item selected and the one below it in the hierarchy.

    • If a survey is selected, you can add another survey or a survey event to that survey.

    • If a survey event is selected, you can add another survey event or a sample to that survey event.

    • If a sample is selected, you can add another sample or a taxon or biotope observation to that sample.

    • If an observation is selected, you can add another observation.

  • The [Add], [Edit] and [Delete] buttons will be disabled, and the detail panel on the right hand side of the window will be activated. You can now fill in the prompts in the right hand pages. You must enter data into the required fields which may be indicated by a coloured background, depending on the settings in the Tools – Options – Appearance screen.

  • When you have made the changes you require, choose [Save] in the right-hand panel of the window to update the database with the new record.

  • You can choose [Cancel] in the right-hand panel to abandon any changes. No changes will be made to the database, and the window will be restored to its state before you started to edit.

Reorganising your Observations

You can move items around within the Observation tree using drag and drop or cut and paste. For a move to be allowed,;

  • You must "own" the item you want to use. I.e. It must have been created on your copy of Recorder or the owner must have passed custodianship onto you .

  • Items must be moved to the correct level in the hierarchy (i.e. Survey events can be moved between Surveys, Samples between Survey events and Taxon or Biotope occurrences between Samples)

  • The item you are trying to move must pass the appropriate validation checks when in its new position.(e.g., the date of a survey event must be within the date range for the survey it is moved to.)

 To move any item around the observation hierarchy using drag and drop

  • Click and hold down the left mouse button on the item that you want to move.

  • The cursor changes to a drag cursor.

  • While holding down the button, drag the item to the position that you want to move it to.

  • Release the mouse button.

  • You will be prompted to confirm that you really want to make the change or informed that the change is not possible and given reasons for this failure.

  • The dragged item has been moved.

To move any item around the observation hierarchy using cut and paste

You may prefer to use this method if you cannot see the original and destination positions of the item at the same time in the hierarchy.

  • Click on the item that you want to move with the right mouse button.

  • Select 'cut'

  • Browse to the new position

  • Right click on the new parent item and select 'paste'

  • You will be prompted to confirm you really want to make the change.

  • The dragged item has been moved.

Note: Don't worry if you find that you cannot paste the item where you want to. Simply make the appropriate change and try again. You won't lose the item you are trying to move. If the 'paste' part of the operation is not completed, the 'cut' will also not occur.
