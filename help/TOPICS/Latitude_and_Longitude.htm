Latitude and Longitude

Latitude and longitude co-ordinates are specified by a pair of real numbers of degrees separated by a comma and one or more spaces. The first number can be followed by "N" or "S" and the second by "E" or "W". Letters are automatically forced into upper case. If no letters are given, "N" and "E" are assumed.

For example the following are equivalent:

"51.4N, 1.43W" - Recorder converts this to "51.4N; 1.43W"

"51.4, -1.43" - Recorder won't accept this format

"51.4N, -1.43E" - Recorder converts this to "51.4N; 1.43W"

The latitudes and longitudes stored by Recorder 6 use the OSGB36 spheroid. This may give slightly different results from the WGS84 spheroid commonly used by GPS systems. In general the lat/long co-ordinates should not differ by more than about 15m within the British Isles, but much larger discrepancies may occur elsewhere on the planet.
