Grid references and spurious accuracy

Consider the following map image.

This image is approximately 300 pixels wide and 500 high, and represents an area on the ground about 800 km east to west and 1,300 km north to south. Therefore, an individual pixel on the screen represents an area nearly 3km square on the ground. Taking this into account, it is unrealistic to derive a grid reference that is more precise than a 10km square (e.g. NZ16) when you click on the map to obtain a spatial reference. However, the software is quite capable of generating a spatial reference to the nearest metre (e,g, NZ 12345 67890). If this was allowed, it would be a case of 'spurious accuracy'.

Recorder uses the scale of the map displayed in the Map Browser to calculate the size on the ground represented by a pixel. This information is used to decide the appropriate precision for the spatial reference that is shown when you click on the map. So, as you zoom in, the spatial reference shown when you click becomes more precise.
