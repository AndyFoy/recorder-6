Two digit years

If you enter a date with the year indicated by one or two digits (e.g. 13/4/99), then the way the date is interpreted depends upon the 'Century cut-off year' (set in the Tools - Options - General window). Rules have been devised which cause the date to be interpreted in an intuitive way.

In detail, these rules are as follows:

  • If the current year (ignoring the century) is less than or equal to the Century cut-off year then:

    • If the year from the date that was entered is less than or equal to the Century cut-off year, then the current century is assumed.

    • If the year from the date that was entered is greater than the Century cut-off year, then the last century is assumed.

  • If the current year (ignoring the century) is greater than the Century cut-off year then:

    • If the year from the date that was entered is less than or equal to the Century cut-off year, then the next century is assumed.

    • If the year from the date that was entered is greater than the Century cut-off year, then the current century is assumed.

Examples:

Assume that the Windows date settings are for European style dates (i.e. day/month/year) and the separator character is '/'.

Today's date: 01/04/2002
Century cut-off year = 40

You type:

Date interpreted as:

13/4/99

13 April 1999

13/4/1

13 April 2001

Today's date: 01/04/2057
Century cut-off year = 40

You type:

Date interpreted as:

13/4/54

13 April 2054

13/4/1

13 April 2101
