Bounding Boxes

A bounding box is a rectangular area defined by two spatial co-ordinates at the south-west and north-east corners (see Geo Info - Location boundaries). They are used for two purposes:

To define the geographical coverage of a Survey:

  • In Recorder, the bounding box for a survey is used to check that the spatial references for survey events and samples associated with that survey are within the bounding box (see spatial reference validation).

To retrieve information:

  • In the Report Wizard, bounding boxes can be used in a 'Report about a place' to find all locations within the bounding box.

  • The 'Restrict by bounding box' option can be used to find all taxon or biotope observations within the bounding box.

  • Choose the button in the bounding box prompt, and the Map window will be displayed with the cursor shown as a cross-hair.

  • Draw a rectangle by clicking at one of the corners of the desired rectangle. Then drag your mouse to the opposite corner while holding down the primary mouse button. A rectangle will be drawn connecting the point at which you clicked with the current mouse position.

  • When you have completed the rectangle, release the mouse button.

  • Return the SW and NE corner co-ordinates of the rectangle you have drawn by:

    • Selecting Edit - Return data from the Main menu.

    • Clicking the button in the active toolbar.
    • Pressing the short-cut key <F9>.
