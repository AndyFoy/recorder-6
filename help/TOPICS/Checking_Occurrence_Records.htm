Checking observations

When an observation is marked as 'Checked', it means that the information stored in the database has been compared with the original (e.g. checked against the record card), preferably by somebody other than the person who made the data entry, and is an accurate transcription. It is not intended to imply that the original information was necessarily correct, only that it has been correctly entered.

Note: If an observation is NOT flagged as Checked:

  • It will not be exported in certain circumstances - see the notes in red in Exports.

  • It will not appear in reports unless you specifically indicate that unchecked observations should be included (Reports – Wizard – Additional filters – Constraints.

To mark observations as checked:

  • Select the taxon or biotope observation in the hierarchy on the left.

  • Choose [Edit] to put the detail panel into Edit mode.
  • Click on the 'checked' box in the General page so that a tick appears in it.
  • Choose [Save] to update the database.

OR

  • Click on the box to the left of the taxon or biotope name in the hierarchy in the left hand panel so that a tick appears in it.

OR

You can mark all the observations in a sample in one operation by:

  • Selecting the sample in the hierarchy on the left.

  • Clicking the [Check All] button at the bottom.
  • You will be prompted to confirm that you want to mark ALL the occurrences for that sample as Checked.
