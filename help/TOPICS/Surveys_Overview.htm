Surveys

In Recorder, all observations must be related to a survey. The survey provides information about the organisation or individual that organised the work, the time period over which it was done, the geographic area that it covered, and its status (on-going, completed, etc.). If the survey resulted in publications, or you have other material about it stored on your computer, then 'Sources' can provide links to this information.

If you wish to contribute information to the National Biodiversity Network (NBN), it will be necessary to supply 'meta-data' . The survey entry provides this type of data.

In the NBN data model, all observations form part of a survey. When data are collected for a particular recording scheme, or as part of a habitat survey or monitoring programme, the survey to which it belongs will usually be obvious. The survey entry for professionally run schemes should ideally be prepared by the organisers of the survey, and distributed to participants in NBN transfer format for import into their copy. However, for old data, those managing the data will need to decide how best to organise it into surveys and prepare suitable entries. Many individuals will probably regard their own personal records, not made as part of any organised scheme, as a single, ongoing survey called 'x’s personal records'.

Note: surveys in the observation hierarchy can be organised into folders by assigning one or more Tags to them. This facility becomes available when Organise Surveys by Tag is ticked on the General tab in Tools - Options. The folders appear as the top level in the hierarchy and the folder names are the tags. The Tags can be created and assigned to surveys in the observation hierarchy. They can also be created via Tools - Enhanced Termlists. Tags enable groups of surveys to be exported and included in reports more easily.
