Cut-In and Cut-Out scales

Digital maps are designed to be viewed at a particular scale. This is determined by the scale of the base map that was digitised or scanned to produce the digital version. While it is quite possible to use them effectively over a range of scales above and below their ideal scale, there comes a point in either direction at which they are no longer useful.

For example: consider an outline map of Great Britain and an OS 1:50,000 map tile (as supplied by the OS, these cover an area of 20x20km). If you view the whole of the GB outline on screen (approx. 700km wide and 1,200km high), then the OS tile will appear as a square blob only a few pixels across. Conversely, if you zoom in so that the OS tile fills the screen, the GB outline – which will probably have been digitised at a fairly coarse scale so that it contains a manageable number of co-ordinates – will probably be a rather poor fit to the coast (i.e. it won't follow all the little bays and inlets shown on the 1:50,000 map, but will consist of a few straight line segments that follow the general shape).

The solution is to turn off the display of the map-tile while you are looking at the whole of GB, and turn off the display of the GB outline while you are zoomed in on the map tile. You can achieve this by setting the scales at which background map layers should 'Cut-in' and 'Cut-out' in the Map Options - Map Layers page. A range of scales from 1:100,000,000 to 1:1 are available.

You will probably find that a degree of trial and error will be necessary to establish settings which work well for a particular set of maps.
