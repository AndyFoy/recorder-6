Export Management

If you need to export the same group of records on a regular basis, the Export Management facility allows you to automate much of the process. Export Management is accessible through Tools on the main menu.

There are two elements involved in export management;

Export filters

The export filter is a definition of the records that should be included in the export. E.g. All otter records in Wales.

    • Export filters can be saved and then used on their own as the basis of an export at a later time.

    • Export filters can created and then sent from one person to another. This means that someone can work out what data they need, and simply send the filter to the data supplier. It is then a simple process for the data provider to make the appropriate export and send it out based on the request.

    • Export filters can also be used as the basis for a Recording Scheme.

Recording Schemes

Quite often, an export of records needs to be sent to the same person on a regular basis. E.g. All bird records sent to the county bird recorder once a month. A Recording Scheme defines the export filter, the data format to be used and the e-mail address that the records should be sent to. The monthly submission can then be made at the touch of a button. Recorder finds observations in the Export filter which have been entered or changed since your last submission. It exports only them, zips the resulting file and e-mails it to the designated E-mail address. The time and date is automatically recorded, so that only new information is sent next time.

Note: Alternatively, an export filter is not defined and all records are included in the recording scheme.
