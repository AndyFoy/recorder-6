Vague dates

It is common, when dealing with biological information, for ranges of dates to be given (e.g. the period for which a trap was set), or that dates are not given precisely. The latter is especially likely when dealing with material from older collections or extracting information from literature. To cope with these situations, Recorder can accept dates in the following formats:

Type

Example

Explanation

Standard date

23 Mar 1987

Normal date – this is the most frequently used form.

Standard date range

23 Mar 1987 - 30 Mar 1987

Useful for survey events when a visit to a location extended over a period of days, or for samples when a trap was set for a period.

Month

Mar 1987

Useful for older, or data extracted from literature, when precise information is not given.

Month range

Mar 1987 – Jun 1987

Useful for older, or data extracted from literature, when precise information is not given. Enter the name of the month (first 3 letters are sufficient), or a roman numeral.

Season

Summer 1987

Useful for older, or data extracted from, literature, when precise information is not given. Enter the name of the season (the first three letters are sufficient).

Year

1987

After standard dates, this is probably the most useful for older data when no precise date is given.

Year range

1981 – 1987

Useful for older, or data extracted from literature, when precise information is not given. Use this form for things like '1960s' (i.e. 1960-1969).

Open ended year range

1987 -

NOTE: Try to avoid using this format.

It is provided for compatibility with pre-existing computerised data only. When entering new data you should always give the range an end (i.e. the current year if nothing else is available).

Up to year

- 1987

Useful when very limited information is available for old specimens of data extracted from literature. For example, if all you know is the date of publication of the work from which the data was extracted, or the date at which the collector died.

Month only

July

Intended for old data extracted from literature where very limited information on flight periods, flowering times etc. may be quoted. Enter the name of the month (first 3 letters are sufficient), or a roman numeral.

Season only

Summer

Intended for old data extracted from literature where very limited information on flight periods, flowering times etc. may be quoted. Enter the name of the season (the first three letters are sufficient).

Unknown

Unknown

Dates are often REQUIRED. If no date information is available then 'Unknown' should be stated explicitly because the date field cannot be left empty. Enter 'U'.

Seasons are defined as follows:

Winter

Dec, Jan, Feb

Spring

Mar, Apr, May

Summer

Jun, Jul, Aug

Autumn

Sep, Oct, Nov
