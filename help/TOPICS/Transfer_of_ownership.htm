Transfer of ownership

It is very important for version control issues that only a single copy of a record is "live" and therefore editable. If edits are possible from a variety of places, the original recorder would have no control over what details were put against their name. Also, if multiple versions of the same record record are around, it becomes very difficult to establish what the "original" and "approved edits" are and what are erroneous.

For these reasons, records are only editable on their owners copy of Recorder. By default, the owner is the originator of the record. There may however be situations where ownership and editing rights need to be transferred to someone else

  • A recorder who has decided to stop recording may donate their dataset to their local record centre or recording scheme. Obviously it is appropriate for the editing rights to be passed over as well.

  • A contractor may do a piece of work and hand over their resulting records.

  • A recorder may pass their data to their county recorder for inclusion in a collated database and be happy to pass ownership as well.

In such cases, it is possible to transfer ownership to the appropriate copy of the programme.

Note:If Users give away ownership of their data, they will lose the ability to edit those records. This can only be returned to them by the person they have given the ownership to.

To transfer ownership you need to know the 8 figure Site ID or copy identifier of the copy of Recorder you are transferring ownership to;

  • Open Recorder 6 (the copy of the programme that you are transferring ownership to)

  • Select Help-Summary Info

  • Take a note of the copy identifier.

You may choose to transfer ownership of your entire database

Alternatively, you may choose to transfer ownership of all of the data in a particular export using either the standard export technique or export using a filter.
