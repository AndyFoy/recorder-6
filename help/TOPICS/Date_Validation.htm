Date validation

  • Entries must be valid dates. For example, 31/11/1999 or 29/2/1999 are not valid dates.

  • In most circumstances, dates after today's date are not acceptable. You cannot specify that an observation was made, a specimen was determined, or a site was designated at a date in the future! There are a few exceptions to this: for example, you are allowed to set the end date for a survey into the future.

  • Where 'From' and 'To' dates are used, the date given in the 'From' prompt must be before the date given in the 'To' prompt.

  • Dates must be consistent with each other. For example, if you gave start and end dates for a survey, then dates given for the survey events and samples within that survey must be between the start and end dates you specified for the whole survey.

Troubleshooting

If you are having problems with dates that appear perfectly valid being rejected, check the Regional settings in your Windows control panel, since this is where Windows programs get the information on how to interpret dates. The two most likely problems are:

  • The order of elements (day, month, year): For example, 31/3/1999 may or may not be valid, depending on the expected ordering of elements. If your Regional settings are for the American pattern of 'm/d/y' (i.e month number, then day number, then year) then this is not a valid date, but if the settings are for the European pattern 'd/m/y', then it is valid.

  • The Regional settings also specify the separator to be used between each element. If you selected 'English (British)' or 'English (United Kingdom)' in the 'Regional settings' tab, and have not changed the default, the separator will be "/".

Another possible problem is inadvertently setting a date into the future because of the way that years entered with one or two digits are interpreted.
