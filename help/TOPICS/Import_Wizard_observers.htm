Import Wizard observers

The Import Wizard processes the contents of the Observer(s) column by splitting up the contents into separate names so that each can be listed as a separate recorder on the survey events and samples. The accepted separators are , ; / \ "and" & +. Because of the variations in the way the separators are used in normal usage problems have arisen with the splitting of the Observer(s) into separate names. Below are some examples that are handled correctly. If your import file contains names in formats not handled correctly by the wizard, you should convert them to another format. You can try importing them and check whether they are split correctly in the window that enables you to match the Observers to individuals in your Names and Addresses list. If the split is not satisfactory you will need to abort the import and edit the import file.

Examples

Observer(s) column

Handled as:

Alice Jones and John North

Alice Jones, John North

Mr and Mrs D Smith

Mr D Smith, Mrs D Smith

Dr G R and K Wood

Dr G R Wood, K Wood

Richard & Susan Brown, John North

Richard Brown, Susan Brown, John North

T & R Green

T Green, R Green

Wood, Mr J.C., Green, R.A.

Mr J.C. Wood, R.A. Green

Jones, Alice, Smith, David

Alice Jones, David Smith

Note: Names containing apostrophes and hyphens are handled correctly when initials precede the surname but not when they follow the surname. For example, "O'Sullivan, I.K. and Taylor-Wilson, N.V." will be split into 4 names: "O'Sullivan" and "I.K.", "Taylor-Wilson" and "N.V." In this case O'Sullivan and I.K. can both be matched to I.K O'Sullivan and the import wizard will import the observations correctly, but then both will be logged as remembered matches in IW_Matched_Names which may cause problems in future imports. Likewise with Taylor-Wilson, N.V.
