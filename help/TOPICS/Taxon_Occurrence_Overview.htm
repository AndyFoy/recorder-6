Taxon Occurrence Overview

A taxon occurrence stores details about the observation of a particular taxon in a sample. This is usually an observation of a species, but it could be any rank of taxon available from the dictionary – a family, a variety, etc. Important information includes the name of the taxon (including from which checklist the name came), who determined it, any measurements that were made (e.g. abundance, density), and details of any specimens that were retained. Occurrences can also be related. For example, observations of a predator and its prey could be linked together.
