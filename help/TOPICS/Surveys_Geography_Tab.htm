Surveys Geography Tab

This page is used to describe the geographic coverage of the survey. It is possible to indicate this by specifying a bounding box (the NE and SW spatial references of a rectangular area of the map), and/or by entering some text describing the coverage.

 

North-East corner

Spatial reference for the north-east corner of a rectangular area encompassing all samples contributing to this survey.

South-West corner

Spatial reference for the south-west corner of a rectangular area encompassing all samples contributing to this survey.

These spatial references can be typed in, but the easiest way to obtain them is to click the button, which displays the Map browser window, and then to draw the rectangle on the map. See Bounding boxes.

  • The cursor will be shown as a crosshairs .

  • Click with the mouse at one corner of the desired rectangle and hold down the primary mouse button.

  • Drag the mouse to the opposite corner – a rectangle will be drawn as you move the mouse. When the area is correctly shown, release the mouse button.

  • Return the two corner references by:

    • Selecting Edit – Return data from the Main menu.

    • Clicking the button on the active toolbar.
    • Pressing the short-cut key <F9>.
    • The NE corner reference must be further north and east than the SW corner reference.

Geographic coverage

RTF Memo. A description of the geographic area covered by the survey.

NOTE:

  • At least one of these two types of entry (bounding box or textual description) is REQUIRED.

  • If a bounding box is given, it is used to validate spatial references given for survey events and samples attached to the survey. A check is made to ensure they are within the bounding box.
