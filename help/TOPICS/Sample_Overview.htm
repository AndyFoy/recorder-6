Sample

A sample record describes a particular sample which was made during the survey event to which it is attached. Several different samples may be made during one survey event. This might involve employing the same sampling method at several places (e.g. throwing several quadrats), or could involve using several different methods at the same place (e.g. making observations along a transect, recording species coverage in a quadrat and setting a pitfall trap). Each distinct method employed by a particular person at a distinct place should be treated as a separate sample.

Samples result in observations of species (taxon occurrences) and/or habitats (biotope occurrence). Typically many species observations may result, but only one habitat observation.

The information contained in a sample record includes the sampling method, who made the sample, where it was made and the time. The location and date default to those given for the survey event to which the sample is attached, but it is often possible (and desirable) to be more precise. For example, the locality given for a survey event may be the nature reserve that was visited, but individual samples can be localised to particular management compartments (perhaps set up as sub-sites in the Locations window) or precise grid references. Similarly, the time element can be more precise and might include the start time and duration of the sample.

A variety of measurements might also be made at a sampling point, for example the vegetation height might be measured in a quadrat, or the conductivity of the water at the point where aquatic invertebrates were netted. The Measurements page allows these to be recorded.

In the case of ad hoc observations, there will probably be very little information recorded in the sample. The locality, date and recorder will default to those for the survey event, and only the sampling method need be given (probably 'field observation').
