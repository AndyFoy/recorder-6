Additional Filters conditions

This page of the Additional Filters dialog allows additional criteria to be applied to narrow down the observations to go in a report.

To add an additional criterion:

  • Choose the button – the top panel becomes active.

  • Select one of the available fields from the list on the left.

  • Select one of the conditions from the list on the right.

  • Type the value you wish to test for in the Criteria edit box.

    • Choose the button to accept this condition, and add it to the query.

    • Choose if you make a mistake, and decide you don’t want to add the condition.

In the screenshot above, the test being applied is: 'Event Date is Greater than 13/12/1969', i.e. the date of the survey event is from 1970 onwards.

Tip: The available conditions change depending on the type of data.

You can manipulate the conditions as follows:

  • Add more conditions by choosing and selecting field, condition and criterion as explained above. When the second and subsequent condition is added, the column at the left hand side of the list allows you to choose whether to link conditions with 'AND' or 'OR'.

AND

Both conditions must apply.

In the example, the sample date must be after the end of 1969 AND the determiners name must contain the string 'ball' for an observation to be selected.

OR

Either condition can apply.

If OR had been selected in the example above, then observations with dates before 1970 would be included if their determiner’s name contained the string 'ball'. Observations after the end of 1969 would be included, whatever their determiner was called.

  • Remove a condition from the list by highlighting it, and then choosing the button.

  • Remove all conditions from the list by choosing [Clear All].

  • Edit a condition by highlighting it in the list, and choosing . The upper part of the page will be activated, the appropriate available field and condition will be selected, and the criterion will be shown in the edit box. Make the changes and choose to update the condition shown in the list.

  • If there is more than one condition, you can highlight a condition in the list and use the and buttons to alter its position within the list. The order in which conditions are listed is important for correct interpretation of AND and OR. Conditions are processed from top to bottom so:

AND

B

OR

C
