Recording Cards overview

Many national recording schemes and surveys adopt physical recording cards to record observations of taxon or biotopes at a particular location. These physical recording cards are represented electronically in Recorder 2002. Their purposes are:

  • To present the user with a familiar format for recording multiple observations.

  • To provide an efficient method of rapid data entry for taxon at a location.

  • To allow additional information to be easily recorded, such as a taxon count.

  • To allow customisation, so that users can set up and use any number of cards for specific surveys (and transfer them to/from other users).

For many users, the recording card represents the most efficient method for entering multiple taxon observations for a location. For a single taxon observation, the Enter a species observation should be used.

The information on a recording card is stored as a new sample for the selected survey. Where the sample is stored depends on the location, date and recorder for the recording card. If a survey event already exists with the same location, date and recorder as the recording card, then the card is added as a new sample for that event. However, if no matching event exists, a new survey event is created, and the card added as a new sample for this event.
