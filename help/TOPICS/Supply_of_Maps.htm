Supply of maps

Obtaining digitised maps

Although digitised maps (both vector and raster) and satellite photographs are freely available in the USA, in Britain and most of the rest of Europe they are not, and must be purchased. The cost of digital maps has probably been the main constraint that has prevented much wider use and availability of Geographical Information Systems (GIS).

It is worth searching American web sites. High quality, ortho-rectified satellite images of Europe, including parts of Britain, can be found for free download. There are freely available digital maps covering the whole world (e.g. coastal outlines, admin. boundaries, roads, rivers, etc). These are usually based on Lat./Long. or UTM co-ordinate systems.

In Britain and Ireland, the main supplier of large scale maps is the Ordnance Survey. Their web site contains information on products, prices and availability. The series of 1:50,000 and 1:10,000 map tiles are probably of most interest to biological recorders (1:25,000 are not yet available electronically). However, there are many other suppliers for smaller scale maps covering the whole country or substantial regions. Bartholemew, Harper-Collins and the AA all have web sites where you will find details of digital maps. There are many agencies who offer services in producing and supplying ortho-rectified aerial photographs and satellite images.

Note: Since the above was written, the NBN has negotiated an agreement with Ordnance Survey to enable Recorder users to use the maps from the 1:50,000 Scale Colour Raster dataset without having to buy individual map tiles from OS - see OS 1:50,000 Map tiles for NBN use.
