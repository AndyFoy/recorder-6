Biotope

A biotope is defined* as:

"a particular area which is substantially uniform in its environmental conditions and flora and fauna."

A habitat is defined* as:

"the particular environment inhabited by a particular living organism. The environment thus described comprises the whole complex of fauna, flora, soil and climatic factors to which the organism is adapted."

Thus biotope focuses on the nature of an area whereas habitat focuses on the relationship of a particular organism to its environment. However, the term "habitat" is often extended to cover the environment inhabited by a community of organisms and the two terms become virtually synonymous.

Since the majority of classification systems dealt with in biological recording attempt to classify areas of land, often based on their vegetation and environmental conditions, and sometimes their fauna as well, the term "biotope classification" is more appropriate than "habitat classification".

*A Dictionary of Birds. Ed. Campbell & Lack, 1985, Poyser.
